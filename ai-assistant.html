What is the derivative of x² and why is it 2x?
👤
🤖

Great question! Let me explain step by step:

The derivative of a function tells us the rate of change at any point.

Using the Power Rule:

d/dx [xⁿ] = n·xⁿ⁻¹

For x²: n = 2

d/dx [x²] = 2·x²⁻¹ = 2x

Why 2x? It means at any point x, the slope of the curve x² is exactly 2x. For example:

  • At x = 1, slope = 2
  • At x = 3, slope = 6
  • At x = 0, slope = 0 (the curve is flat)
